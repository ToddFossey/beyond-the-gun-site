What This Book Confronts

Beyond the Gun marks a crucial pivot point in how Armed Guardians must think about training. Written from the perspective of professional human performance coach turned firearms training innovator Todd Fossey, this book challenges deeply held assumptions about how people learn, retain, and perform life-saving skills under stress.

Drawing from violent crime statistics, force science, and motor-learning research, Fossey exposes why many traditional training methods create the illusion of preparedness rather than durable, transferable capability.

Real learning—learning that holds up when conditions change and pressure rises—is rarely comfortable. It is shaped by how humans adapt, process information, and respond to uncertainty. Training must be aligned with the realities in which those skills may ultimately be required.

This is not a technical manual or a collection of drills. It is a direct, impassioned, and intellectually grounded exploration of what it means to prepare responsibly for real-world violence—equipping readers to rethink how they train, why they train, and what must change if skills are to be accessible when lives are on the line.

You will never look at personal protection training the same way again.

About Todd Fossey

Founder. Instructor. Systems thinker.

Todd Fossey is the founder and chief instructor of Integrative Defense Strategies (IDS) and the creator of the IDS APEX Training System. His work challenges conventional firearms training through the lens of force science, behavioral science, skill acquisition, and real-world performance under pressure.

Beyond the Gun extends that mission in book form—offering a sharper framework for defenders, coaches, and serious students who want training that holds up when it matters most.
